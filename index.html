About BitForge

BitForge is an AI-driven hardware design automation startup co-founded by Rohil Khare (ex-Amazon) and Tamzid Razzaque (ex-Apple). Our mission is to revolutionize hardware development by automating HDL code generation, debugging, synthesis, and verification using advanced AI.

With decades of combined experience in hardware design at leading tech companies, our team understands the challenges of hardware development firsthand. We're backed by Microsoft for Startups and use Azure AI Foundry for scalable cloud-based inference.

Our vision is to make hardware design more accessible, efficient, and iterative by leveraging the latest advancements in artificial intelligence and machine learning.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does BitForge compare to traditional EDA tools?

BitForge complements traditional EDA tools by automating many of the manual coding and debugging tasks. We integrate with existing workflows while providing AI-powered capabilities that traditional tools lack. Our extension works alongside tools like Cadence, Vivado, and others to enhance your productivity.

What hardware description languages do you support?

BitForge currently supports Verilog and VHDL, with plans to add SystemVerilog and other HDLs in the near future. Our AI models are trained on extensive code repositories and understand industry-standard coding practices.

Is my code secure with BitForge?

Yes. We take security seriously. Your code never leaves your machine unless you explicitly choose cloud-based features. For Enterprise customers, we offer fully isolated deployments and private LLM instances to ensure maximum data security and IP protection.

Can BitForge handle complex designs?

Absolutely. Our AI has been trained on millions of lines of HDL code, including complex designs from various domains. While the complexity ceiling continues to increase as we improve our models, BitForge can currently handle designs ranging from simple controllers to complex processors and specialized accelerators.

Do I need to be online to use BitForge?

Some features like code visualization work offline, but our AI-powered capabilities require an internet connection to access our cloud inference servers. Enterprise customers have the option for on-premises deployment for sensitive projects.

How often is BitForge updated?

We release updates on a regular cadence, typically every 2-4 weeks for minor improvements and bug fixes. Major feature releases occur quarterly. Our AI models are continuously trained and improved based on user feedback and new data.
