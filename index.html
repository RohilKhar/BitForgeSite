Frequently Asked Questions

How does Sigmantic compare to traditional EDA tools?

Sigmantic isn't here to replace your EDA tools — it supercharges them. Unlike traditional EDA suites that rely heavily on manual workflows, Sigmantic uses AI to generate, annotate, debug, and test HDL code faster and more accurately. It seamlessly integrates with your existing toolchain to accelerate development without disrupting your current setup.

What hardware description languages do you support?

We currently support Verilog, SystemVerilog, and VHDL. Support for emerging DSLs and high-level synthesis tools is on our roadmap, and we're actively expanding language compatibility based on user needs.

Is my code secure with Sigmantic?

Yes — security and privacy are core to our platform. Your code never leaves your private workspace unless explicitly shared. We offer end-to-end encryption, and enterprise users can deploy Sigmantic on-prem or in a VPC for full control.

Can Sigmantic handle complex designs?

Absolutely. Sigmantic is built to scale — whether you're prototyping a simple FSM or designing a complex pipelined SoC. Our AI assistants understand structural hierarchy, pipelining, parameterization, and reuse patterns in large codebases.

Do I need to be online to use Sigmantic?

Sigmantic is cloud-native, but we offer offline support for enterprise users. While our core AI tools run in the cloud for real-time assistance and updates, on-prem deployment options are available for teams with strict air-gapped environments.

How often is Sigmantic updated?

Sigmantic is updated regularly, and all updates are pushed directly through the VSCode extension. No manual setup required — you'll always be working with the latest features and improvements seamlessly within your existing workflow.
